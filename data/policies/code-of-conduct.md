# Code of Conduct

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Policy Owner:** Human Resources Department
**Last Updated:** January 15, 2026
**Applies To:** All employees, contractors, interns, and temporary workers

## 1. Purpose

This Code of Conduct defines the standards of behavior expected of everyone at Northstar Foods. It reflects our commitment to maintaining a professional, respectful, and inclusive workplace.

## 2. Core Values

- **Integrity:** Act honestly and transparently in all interactions.
- **Respect:** Treat all colleagues, clients, and partners with dignity.
- **Accountability:** Take responsibility for your actions and decisions.
- **Collaboration:** Work together to achieve shared goals.
- **Innovation:** Embrace creativity and continuous improvement.

## 3. Professional Behavior

- Employees must maintain professional conduct in all work settings, including remote and virtual environments.
- Punctuality and reliability are expected for meetings, deadlines, and commitments.
- Disagreements must be handled constructively and respectfully.
- Gossip, rumor-spreading, and undermining colleagues are not tolerated.

## 4. Anti-Discrimination and Harassment

- Northstar Foods has zero tolerance for discrimination based on race, gender, age, disability, sexual orientation, religion, national origin, or any other protected characteristic.
- Harassment of any kind — verbal, physical, visual, or digital — is strictly prohibited.
- All reports of discrimination or harassment will be investigated promptly and confidentially.
- Retaliation against anyone who reports misconduct in good faith is prohibited.

## 5. Conflicts of Interest

- Employees must disclose any personal, financial, or professional relationships that could create a conflict of interest.
- Outside employment must be approved by the employee's manager and HR.
- Employees must not use company resources for personal benefit.

## 6. Confidentiality

- Employees must protect confidential company information during and after employment.
- Sharing proprietary information with unauthorized parties is grounds for immediate termination.
- NDAs must be signed as part of the onboarding process.

## 7. Reporting Violations

- Violations can be reported to:
  - Direct manager
  - HR department: **hr@companyx.com**
  - Anonymous ethics hotline: **ethics.companyx.com**
- All reports are treated confidentially.
- Northstar Foods prohibits retaliation against reporters.

## 8. Consequences

- Violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.
- Severity of consequences depends on the nature and frequency of the violation.
- Serious violations (theft, fraud, violence, harassment) may result in immediate termination and legal action.
