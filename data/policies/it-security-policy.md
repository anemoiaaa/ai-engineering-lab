# IT Security Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Policy Owner:** Information Technology Department
**Last Updated:** March 10, 2026
**Applies To:** All employees, contractors, and third-party vendors with access to company systems

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the security requirements for the use of Northstar Foods's information technology resources. All personnel must comply with these requirements to protect company data, systems, and infrastructure.

## 2. Password Requirements

- Minimum **14 characters** with at least one uppercase letter, one lowercase letter, one number, and one special character.
- Passwords must be changed every **90 days**.
- Password reuse is prohibited for the last **12 passwords**.
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is **mandatory** for all company accounts.

## 3. Device Security

### 3.1 Company-Issued Devices
- All company devices must have full-disk encryption enabled.
- Automatic screen lock must be set to activate after **5 minutes** of inactivity.
- Only IT-approved software may be installed on company devices.
- Devices must be returned within **5 business days** of employment termination.

### 3.2 BYOD (Bring Your Own Device)
- Personal devices used for work must be registered with IT and comply with the company MDM (Mobile Device Management) policy.
- BYOD devices must have up-to-date antivirus software and OS patches.
- Company data on BYOD devices may be remotely wiped upon employment termination.

## 4. Network Security

- VPN must be used when connecting to company resources from outside the office.
- Employees must not connect to unsecured public Wi-Fi networks for work purposes.
- The IT department monitors network traffic for security threats. Personal use of company networks must comply with the Acceptable Use Policy.

## 5. Data Classification

| Classification | Description | Examples |
|----------------|-------------|----------|
| **Public** | Information intended for public distribution | Marketing materials, press releases |
| **Internal** | Information for internal use only | Meeting notes, internal presentations |
| **Confidential** | Sensitive business information | Financial reports, strategic plans |
| **Restricted** | Highly sensitive data requiring strict controls | PII, trade secrets, credentials |

## 6. Incident Reporting

- All security incidents must be reported to the IT Security team within **1 hour** of discovery.
- Report incidents via: **security@companyx.com** or the **#security-incidents** Slack channel.
- Do not attempt to investigate or remediate incidents independently.
- Failure to report known security incidents may result in disciplinary action.

## 7. Software and Access

- Software installation requests must be submitted through the **IT Service Desk**.
- Access to systems and data follows the **principle of least privilege**.
- Access reviews are conducted **quarterly** by department managers.
- All access is revoked within **24 hours** of employment termination.

## 8. Email and Communication

- Company email must not be used for personal purposes.
- Employees must not open attachments or click links from unknown or suspicious senders.
- Phishing simulation tests are conducted monthly. Employees who fail three consecutive tests must complete additional security training.
