# Expense Reimbursement Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Policy Owner:** Finance Department
**Last Updated:** February 1, 2026
**Applies To:** All employees authorized to incur business expenses

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines the guidelines for incurring, documenting, and claiming reimbursement for business-related expenses.

## 2. General Principles

- Expenses must be **reasonable, necessary, and directly related** to company business.
- Employees must obtain **pre-approval** from their manager for expenses exceeding **$200**.
- All expenses must be supported by **original receipts** or digital copies.

## 3. Eligible Expenses

### 3.1 Travel
- Economy/coach class for flights under 6 hours.
- Business class may be approved for flights over 6 hours with VP-level approval.
- Hotel accommodations up to **$200/night** (domestic) or **$300/night** (international).
- Ground transportation: taxi, rideshare, or rental car (compact or midsize category).
- Mileage reimbursement for personal vehicle use: **$0.67/mile** (IRS 2026 rate).

### 3.2 Meals
- Per diem meal allowance: **$75/day** (domestic) or **$100/day** (international).
- Client entertainment meals: up to **$150/person** with prior manager approval.
- Alcohol is reimbursable only during client entertainment, limited to **2 drinks per person**.

### 3.3 Office Supplies
- Supplies under **$50** may be purchased without pre-approval.
- Supplies over $50 must go through the procurement process.

### 3.4 Professional Development
- Conference registration, courses, and certifications up to **$2,000/year** per employee.
- Must be pre-approved by manager and aligned with the employee's development plan.
- Travel costs for professional development follow standard travel guidelines.

## 4. Non-Eligible Expenses

- Personal items and entertainment (unless client-related).
- Traffic fines, parking tickets, or legal fees.
- First-class travel upgrades.
- Expenses for family members or non-employees.
- Gift cards or cash equivalents.

## 5. Submission Process

- Expense reports must be submitted within **30 days** of incurring the expense.
- Submit via the **Expense Management System** (accessible through the HR Portal).
- Required information: date, vendor, amount, business purpose, receipt attachment.
- Expenses submitted after 60 days may be denied.

## 6. Approval and Payment

- Manager approval required within **5 business days** of submission.
- Finance processes approved expenses within **10 business days**.
- Reimbursement is paid via direct deposit to the employee's payroll account.

## 7. Corporate Credit Card

- Corporate cards are available for employees who travel frequently (3+ trips per quarter).
- Personal use of corporate credit cards is strictly prohibited and may result in termination.
- Monthly statements must be reconciled within **10 business days**.
