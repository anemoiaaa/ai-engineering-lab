# Performance Review Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Policy Owner:** Human Resources Department
**Last Updated:** January 20, 2026
**Applies To:** All full-time employees past the probationary period

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines the performance evaluation framework at Northstar Foods. Regular performance reviews ensure employees receive feedback, recognize achievements, and identify growth opportunities.

## 2. Review Cycle

| Review Type | Frequency | When |
|-------------|-----------|------|
| Annual Performance Review | Yearly | January (for the previous calendar year) |
| Mid-Year Check-In | Yearly | July |
| Quarterly Goals Review | Quarterly | End of each quarter |
| Probationary Review | Once | At 90 days of employment |

## 3. Rating Scale

| Rating | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **5 — Exceptional** | Consistently exceeds all expectations; demonstrates extraordinary impact |
| **4 — Exceeds Expectations** | Frequently exceeds expectations in key areas |
| **3 — Meets Expectations** | Consistently meets all role requirements and expectations |
| **2 — Needs Improvement** | Falls short of expectations in one or more key areas |
| **1 — Unsatisfactory** | Consistently fails to meet minimum expectations |

## 4. Review Process

### 4.1 Self-Assessment
- Employees complete a self-assessment form covering goals, achievements, challenges, and development areas.
- Self-assessments are due **2 weeks** before the review meeting.

### 4.2 Manager Assessment
- Managers evaluate employees based on:
  - Goal achievement (40%)
  - Core competencies (30%)
  - Company values alignment (20%)
  - Growth and development (10%)

### 4.3 Peer Feedback (360 Review)
- Employees select 3–5 peers for feedback.
- Peer feedback is anonymous and used as supplementary input.
- Participation in peer reviews is mandatory when requested.

### 4.4 Review Meeting
- Manager and employee meet for a **60-minute** review discussion.
- The meeting covers: performance summary, strengths, areas for improvement, and goals for the next period.
- A written summary is shared within **5 business days** of the meeting.

## 5. Goal Setting

- Goals follow the **SMART framework**: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound.
- Each employee should have **3–5 goals** per review period.
- At least one goal should be related to **professional development**.

## 6. Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)

- Employees rated **2 or below** may be placed on a PIP.
- PIPs last **60–90 days** and include specific, measurable improvement targets.
- Regular check-ins (weekly) are required during a PIP.
- Failure to meet PIP targets may result in reassignment or termination.

## 7. Compensation Impact

- Annual performance ratings directly influence **salary adjustments** and **bonus eligibility**.
- Rating of 3 or above: eligible for standard salary increase (typically 3–5%).
- Rating of 4 or above: eligible for performance bonus (typically 5–15% of base salary).
- Rating of 5: eligible for accelerated promotion consideration.
